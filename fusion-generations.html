Fusion Generations: D–T, D–³He, p–¹¹B

Fusion fuels form a progression from easy-but-neutron-heavy D–T, through low-neutron D–³He, to aneutronic p–¹¹B. Kronos stages up this ladder toward ever-cleaner reactions.

Gen 1 — D–T
Easiest; ~80% neutrons
Gen 2 — D–³He
Low-neutron (Kronos baseline)
Gen 3 — p–¹¹B
Aneutronic; hardest (endpoint)
Kronos
Stages up the ladder

It's useful to think of fusion fuels as generations, trading ease of ignition for cleanliness:

GenerationFuel / character
FirstD–T — easiest to ignite, but ~80% neutrons
SecondD–³He — low-neutron, directly convertible (Kronos baseline)
Thirdp–¹¹B — truly aneutronic, hardest

Most of the field is working on the first generation. Kronos commits to the second from the start and stages toward the third — using D–T only for commissioning, running D–³He commercially, and holding p–¹¹B as a gated endpoint. Each step up the ladder trades more physics difficulty for fewer neutrons. See why Kronos skips D–T-first.
